Recent Developments

Data Science for Rural Health in MT: I started the Montana Rural Health Data Science Center as part of my senior undergraduate capstone project. We are a 501c3 focused on using data science and machine learning to benefit health in Montana. Currently, we're developing a model to alert patients of diabetes risk factors, and are deploying a model to detect lung and ovarian cancer in CT scans using topological data analysis.

Fun with machine learning: Lately, I started making models for Numerai, the world's first public hedge fund. I think its an interesting idea. You should check it out.

NCUR 2021: I'll be giving a poster presentation at NCUR 2021 this year on my most recent work in discrete Morse theory. Link to abstract

Web Development: I made this nifty website for a web development course using some similar themes as are used in this site. I rather like it, it's meant to be an instant vacation. Nature break site

CCCG 2020: I attended and presented in the Canadian Conference on Computational Geometry in August 2020. Though it was remote, I still gained a ton from the event and came out even more excited about research and discrete Morse theory. Link to paper

Awards: I was selected as a 2020/21 Outstanding Scholar of the year in the MSU Dept. of Mathematical Sciences. My studies are also supported by the MSU Cameron Presidential Scholarship, the John L. Magaret Scholarship, and the John C. Fulton Scholarship. I was also privileged to win the 2019/20 undergraduate researcher of the year in the MSU school of computing. Thank you to everyone contributing to public education in Montana, your support means everything.
